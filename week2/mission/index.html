Mission Statement

Brigham Young University–Idaho is a privately owned and operated institution of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The University was founded in 1888 as Bannock Stake Academy. It became Ricks College in 1917 and was elevated to university status in 2001.

Guiding Principles

The University's mission is to develop disciples of Jesus Christ who are leaders in their homes, the Church, and their communities.

Four Key Focuses

  • Spiritual Strength: Build testimonies of the restored gospel of Jesus Christ and encourage students to commit to lifelong learning and service.
  • Educational Excellence: Provide a high quality education that prepares students for lifelong learning and employment.
  • Student Access: Make a BYU–Idaho education available to a greater number of Church members throughout the world.
  • Affordability: Keep the cost of education low so that more members of the Church can attend and benefit.
